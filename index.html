ABOUT US

Hi, we are Mark & Angie Gray and 'MGBD Parts' is the part of our business dedicated to keeping Rover P6 cars on the road at cost effective prices.
We have over 30 years experience of owning, repairing, restoring and running Rover P6 cars in all variants, 2000, 2200 & 3500, saloon and estate, both as everyday and show cond ition cars. Our current fleet includes an everyday user 1966 Rover 2000, and also a 1972 Rover 2000TC export, 1975 Rover 2200 Auto, 1972 Rover 3500 Estoura (estate) and of course, the last P6 off the production line 'WC 700S', the avocado Rover 3500S. We have had over 25 years involvement in classic car clubs mostly at committee level and have now turned our specialist knowledge and experience into our Rover P6 parts business 'MGBD Parts', which is dedicated to supplying quality parts for all variants of the Rover P6 built between 1963-1977. The parts stocked are predominantly brand new manufactured items but there is also a mix of 'NOS' (New Old Stock), refurbished service exchange and second hand parts. But if you can't see what you need please email or call us. Also if you need any advice or technical help, we are only a phone call away and we'll be glad to help and what's more, that's free!.

OUR SERVICES

MACHINING & SLEEVING. As well as New Parts for P6â€™s we also refurbish calipers and brake servos. Rear calipers have the hydraulic cylinder machined out and sleeved with stainless steel and this also applies to brake servo valve bodies. Calipers and servos are then rebuilt with all new seals and gaskets which are made especially for us. The machining and sleeving service is also extended to some other cylinders including wheel cylinders for Morris 8, so get in touch if you have any questions.

REFURBISHING. Other refurbished parts include water pumps for Rover 2000 & 2200 both short and long nose and also the Rover 3500.

CHROMING. We have the facility to re-chrome most items for example , bumpers, number plate plinths, overriders, fuel filler caps, wheel nuts, radiator cowls, rocker cover dome nuts, pretty much anything.

However more interesting items have included a guitar finger board, bonnet mascot, Morris 8 windscreen surround and also a 1904 Rover Bicycle. The Rover bicycle was satin matt nickel plate just like it would have been in 1904 and the items done included the seat bar, handle bar, brake mechanism, wheel hubs and rims and a large number of very small nuts and bolts.

POLISHING. The most recent addition to our range is polished rocker covers than make your engine bays gleam.
